Developing Effective Pest and Disease Management Strategies for Rice

Predictive Modeling for Pest and Disease Outbreaks in Rice Fields

Rice is a staple food for over half of the world's population, making its cultivation a critical component of global food security. However, rice production faces significant challenges from pests and diseases, which can cause substantial yield losses and threaten the livelihoods of millions of farmers.

What:- Developing and applying statistical analysis and predictive modeling techniques to forecast pest and disease outbreaks in rice fields.

Where:- In rice-growing regions where pest and disease outbreaks pose significant threats to crop yields and livelihoods.

Who:- Farmers, agricultural scientists, policymakers, and extension services focused on improving rice production and mitigating risks associated with pest and disease outbreaks.

Why:- Early prediction of outbreaks is crucial for timely intervention, reducing crop losses, and ensuring food security. Accurate models can help farmers and agricultural stakeholders make informed decisions, leading to more effective pest and disease management strategies.
